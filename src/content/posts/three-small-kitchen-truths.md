---
title: "Three small kitchen truths"
date: 2026-02-11
tag: living
excerpt: "Things I only learned after about the thousandth dinner. None are revelations. All are the kind of thing a friend might mention at the right moment."
---

One. Salt the water like you mean it, and then don't salt the sauce until the end. The pasta will season itself on the way in. If you reverse this order, you will season it twice, badly.

Two. Most recipes that don't work are a timing problem, not a skill problem. The onions needed another seven minutes. The pan wasn't hot enough. The rest was a wait you didn't take.

Three. The best weeknight move is cooking one thing really well and letting everything else on the plate be honest about what it is: bread is bread, a tomato is a tomato, salt is salt. Assembly is a skill; effort isn't a virtue.

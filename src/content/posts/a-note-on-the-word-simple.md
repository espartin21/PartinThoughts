---
title: "A note on the word 'simple'"
date: 2026-01-18
tag: work
excerpt: "We use 'simple' to mean at least four different things at work, and most of our disagreements are actually about which one."
---

There's simple-to-learn (a first-time user can figure it out). Simple-to-explain (you can describe it in a sentence). Simple-to-build (the implementation has few moving parts). And simple-to-extend (future changes are obvious).

These often trade against each other. A thing that is simple to learn often isn't simple to build; a thing that's simple to build often accumulates weird edges that make it hard to explain later. When two people argue about simplicity, they are usually arguing about which of these to privilege.

## A small habit

When someone says 'we should keep this simple,' I've started asking: simple _for whom_, and _for how long_? It's almost rude to ask, at first. But it turns the conversation from taste into tradeoff, and tradeoffs are things you can actually decide.

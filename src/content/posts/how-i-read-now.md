---
title: "How I read now"
date: 2025-10-02
tag: personal
excerpt: "A small essay about giving up on finishing books, and how it made me a better reader."
---

I used to treat books like contracts. Started means finished. If I was bored I was failing the book, or the book was failing me, and either way someone had to pay.

Now I keep about six going at a time and I read whichever one is calling to me. The ones that stop calling go back on the shelf. Some of them come back. Some don't. The guilt is gone, and — this is the part I didn't expect — the finishing rate went up.
